PDIR Product Bias Correction

Collaborated with Dr. Kuo-Lin-Hsu (UC Irvine) to improve PDIR satellite precipitation data using machine learning and topographical parameters (slope, aspect, elevation).

Work Done: Compared PDIR pixel values with gauge observations and accounted for orographic effects in the Nepal Himalaya.
Achievements: Improved accuracy for light and medium precipitation events; strong seasonal performance.
Key Learnings: Extreme rainfall events remain challenging due to PDIR limitations, missing gauge data, and coarse resolution (4 km × 4 km).
Future Work: Further refinement of correction methods for high-intensity events is ongoing.

Watershed Modelling for Irrigation Sustainability

Conducted watershed modelling in the Kankai and Rapti River basins using SWAT to assess the sustainability of current irrigation projects under different climate scenarios (SSP2-4.5 and SSP5-8.5).

Work Done: Simulated watershed hydrology to study flow conditions under future climate scenarios and developed a model for monsoon-fed spring dynamics.
Findings: SSP2-4.5 projects decreasing runoff; SSP5-8.5 projects higher peak flows but declining baseflow.
Implications: Results highlight the dual challenge of managing floods during monsoon and ensuring irrigation water availability in dry seasons.

Drought Analysis and Agricultural Water Needs

Analyzed the impact of meteorological droughts on Net Irrigation Water Requirement (NIWR) using historical data (1985–2024). Drought indices (SPI, SPEI) were derived and NIWR was simulated with AquaCrop for maize, paddy, and wheat.

Work Done: Compared NIWR during drought and normal seasons; determined the Factor of Safety (FOS) for irrigation systems.
Key Observations: Drought periods significantly increased irrigation water demand compared to normal conditions.
Outcome: Established a clear link between meteorological drought, irrigation requirements, and FOS—providing insights for planning sustainable irrigation projects.
