Some of my Research Works

PDIR Product Bias Correction

Collaborated with Dr. Kuo-Lin-Hsu (UC Irvine) to enhance PDIR satellite precipitation data.

  • Used machine learning and topographical parameters (slope, aspect, elevation).
  • Compared PDIR pixel values with gauge observations in the Nepal Himalaya.
  • Improved accuracy for light and medium precipitation; strong seasonal performance.
  • Learned extreme rainfall is challenging due to PDIR limitations (4 km × 4 km resolution).
  • Ongoing: Refining corrections for high-intensity events.

Watershed Modelling for Irrigation Sustainability

Assessed irrigation sustainability in Kankai and Rapti River basins using SWAT.

  • Simulated hydrology under climate scenarios (SSP2-4.5 & SSP5-8.5).
  • Built a monsoon spring dynamics model.
  • Findings: SSP2-4.5 showed reduced runoff; SSP5-8.5 had higher peak flows, declining baseflow.
  • Highlighted flood management and irrigation challenges.

Drought Analysis and Agricultural Water Needs

Studied meteorological drought impacts on Net Irrigation Water Requirement (NIWR).

  • Used 1985–2024 data, derived SPI/SPEI indices.
  • Simulated NIWR with AquaCrop for maize, paddy, and wheat.
  • Found significant irrigation demand increases during droughts.
  • Linked drought, irrigation needs, and system Factor of Safety (FOS).

Seasonal NIWR Prediction Using Satellite Data

Developed a model for crop NIWR in Chitwan, Nepal (2000–2024).

  • Used AquaCrop and MODIS-based drought indices (LST, ESI).
  • Calibrated Random Forest (R² = 0.83), validated (R² = 0.72), predicted 2020–2023 (R² = 0.74).
  • Enabled early drought detection and improved water planning.
