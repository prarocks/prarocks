Works I've Done

PDIR Product Bias Correction

Collaborated with Dr. Kuo-Lin-Hsu (UC Irvine) to improve PDIR satellite precipitation data using machine learning and topographical parameters (slope, aspect, elevation). Compared PDIR pixel values with gauge observations in the Nepal Himalaya. Improved accuracy for light and medium precipitation with strong seasonal performance. Learned that extreme rainfall remains challenging due to PDIR limitations, missing data, and coarse resolution (4 km × 4 km). Ongoing work focuses on refining corrections for high-intensity events.

Watershed Modelling for Irrigation Sustainability

Conducted watershed modelling in the Kankai and Rapti River basins using SWAT to assess irrigation sustainability under climate scenarios (SSP2-4.5 & SSP5-8.5). Simulated hydrology and built a monsoon spring dynamics model. Found reduced runoff in SSP2-4.5 and higher peak flows but declining baseflow in SSP5-8.5, highlighting dual challenges of flood management in monsoon and irrigation needs in dry seasons.

Drought Analysis and Agricultural Water Needs

Analyzed meteorological drought impacts on Net Irrigation Water Requirement (NIWR) using 1985–2024 data. Derived SPI/SPEI indices and simulated NIWR with AquaCrop for maize, paddy, and wheat. Found significant irrigation demand increases during droughts and established a strong link between meteorological drought, irrigation needs, and system Factor of Safety (FOS).

Seasonal NIWR Prediction Using Satellite Data

Built a seasonal NIWR prediction model (2000–2024) for crops in Chitwan, Nepal using AquaCrop and MODIS-based drought indices (LST, ESI). Calibrated Random Forest (R² = 0.83), validated (R² = 0.72), and predicted NIWR for 2020–2023 (R² = 0.74). The satellite-only approach enabled early drought detection and improved water planning.
