Works I've Done

PDIR Product Bias Correction

Collaborated with Dr. Kuo-Lin-Hsu (UC Irvine) to improve PDIR satellite precipitation data using machine learning and topographical parameters (slope, aspect, elevation).

Work Done: Compared PDIR pixel values with gauge observations, incorporating orographic effects in the Nepal Himalaya.
Achievements: Improved accuracy for light & medium precipitation; strong seasonal performance.
Key Learnings: Extreme rainfall remains challenging due to PDIR limitations, missing data, and coarse resolution (4 km × 4 km).
Future Work: Further refinement of corrections for high-intensity events is ongoing.

Watershed Modelling for Irrigation Sustainability

Conducted watershed modelling in the Kankai and Rapti River basins using SWAT to assess the sustainability of irrigation projects under different climate scenarios (SSP2-4.5 & SSP5-8.5).

Work Done: Simulated hydrology under future climates and built a monsoon-spring dynamics model.
Findings: SSP2-4.5 → reduced runoff; SSP5-8.5 → higher peak flows, but declining baseflow.
Implications: Highlights dual challenges of flood management in monsoon and irrigation needs in dry seasons.

Drought Analysis and Agricultural Water Needs

Analyzed the impact of meteorological droughts on Net Irrigation Water Requirement (NIWR) using historical data (1985–2024). Drought indices (SPI, SPEI) were derived and NIWR simulated with AquaCrop for maize, paddy, and wheat.

Work Done: Compared NIWR in drought vs normal seasons; calculated Factor of Safety (FOS) for irrigation.
Key Observations: Drought periods raised irrigation demand significantly.
Outcome: Established strong link between meteorological drought, irrigation needs, and system FOS.

Seasonal NIWR Prediction Using Satellite Data

Built a seasonal-scale NIWR prediction model (2000–2024) for crops in Chitwan, Nepal using AquaCrop and MODIS-based drought indices (LST, ESI).

Work Done: Calibrated Random Forest (R² = 0.83), validated (R² = 0.72), predicted NIWR 2020–2023 (R² = 0.74).
Motivation: Limited ground data (2016–2019) inspired satellite-only approach.
Application: NIWR trends from MODIS enable early drought detection and better water planning.
